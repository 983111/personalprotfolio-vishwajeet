Interactive Research Paper — Gradient Descent as a Dynamical System
Journal of Optimization Dynamics · Vol. 1, 2025 · Interactive Paper
"Gradient Descent as a Dynamical System: Stability, Phase Transitions, and Convergence"
Fixed Point AnalysisSpectral Stability Phase Transitions4 Interactive Canvas Figures
  • Proved fixed points of the update map Φ_η are exactly the stationary points of L, independent of learning rate η.
  • Derived stability criterion ρ(I − ηH) < 1; optimal η* = 2/(λ_min + λ_max) minimising spectral radius to (κ−1)/(κ+1).
  • Characterised three regimes — geometric convergence, critical oscillation (ρ = 1), explosive divergence — analogous to dynamical bifurcations.
  • Tight convergence bound ‖θ_t − θ*‖ ≤ ρ^t · ‖θ_0 − θ*‖; T(ε) = O(κ · log 1/ε) for optimal step size.
  • Built 4 live simulations: real-time trajectory (Rosenbrock/saddle/quadratic), bifurcation diagram, theory-vs-empirical comparison, complex-plane Jacobian eigenvalue explorer.
  • Discussed momentum as complex eigenvalue rotation yielding O(√κ) convergence improvement; global convergence corollary under L-smoothness and μ-strong convexity.

Dataset
  • Scam URL Dataset (~28,000 URLs): 50% malicious (URLhaus active malware) + 50% benign (Tranco Top 1M); features include IP usage, URL length, executable extensions, special chars, shorteners, risky TLDs.
